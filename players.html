The following are the big players that the Calgary Flames were able to aquire over the offseason, which will be detrimental to their success:
Nazeem Kadri

Playing as the second line center for the Colarado Avalanche last season, kadri was able to elevate his team with his play, resulting in them winning the stanley cup. He was able to use physical play to get under the skin of opposing teams while also driving to the net to produce goals and plays for his team. With Kadri coming into the flames organisation, they finally filled the role of an elite second line center, while making up one of the deepest center groups in the NHL.

Jonathan Huberdeau

While Johnny Gaudreau leaving the flames this offseason hurt them a lot, with the new aquisition of Jonathan Huberdeau they were able to replace Gaudreau entirely. Despite them having different styles of play, both the top line left wingers produced 115 points last season making them tied for second in the league. Huberdeau also provides the leadership that he gave the Florida Panthers over his time there, which Gaudreau failed to do.

Mackenzie Weegar

Mackenzie Weegar was one of the Florida Panthers best defenceman playing on the first pairing. He's great at making transitions through the rush, bringing the puck up, and setting up plays. He plays a great 2 way game and gives the flames some extra fire power from the blueline. With this addition and the defenceman the flames already have, they now have one of the deepest bluelines and arguably the best defensive group in the league.
